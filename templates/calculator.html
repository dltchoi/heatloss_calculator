Heat Loss and Heat Pump Sizing Estimator

Heat pumps are often installed with a back-up heating source. In a hybrid system, that heating source is often a gas furnace. In an all-electric system, it is an electric resistance heating coil. This calculator uses your historical gas utility data to estimate the outdoor temperature where back-up heating would need to kick-in, and the total fraction of your annual heating load that can be met with the heat pump.

Enter the building balance point temperature (in °C):

This is the temperature above which the furnace is no longer required to maintain the indoor temperature set-point. It may be between 12°C and 16°C for the average home. For highly insulated homes or townhomes, it may be lower.

Enter a safety margin for the estimate (as a %):

The calculation algorithm determines the average heat loss, but heat loss can be greater overnight or in windy conditions (or lower during the day or without wind). The safety margin takes adds a small correction to account for the fact that heat loss can be greater than the average in some conditions. A value of 20% or greater is recommended.
